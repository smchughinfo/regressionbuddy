One-sided Limits One-sided limits are only evaluated from one side at at time. Left-hand limits As \( x \rightarrow a \) from the left \( f(x) \rightarrow L \)
\( \lim\limits_{x\to\ a^{-}} f(x)=L \) Right-hand limits As \( x \rightarrow a \) from the right \( f(x) \rightarrow L \)
\( \lim\limits_{x\to\ a^{+}} f(x)=L \) Two Variables Example /images/calculus-appendix/one-sided-limit-example-1.svg https://www.geogebra.org/graphing/qma9H2zd f(n) = \begin{cases} x, & \text{if x < 1} \\ x+1, & \text{if x $\geq$ 1} \end{cases}
