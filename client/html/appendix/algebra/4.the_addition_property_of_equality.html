The Addition Property of Equality If \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are real numbers then: \(a=b\) and \( a+c=b+c\) are equivalent equations. Example 1 \( \phantom{+2 \:\:} x - 2 = 3 \)
\( x - 2 \color{#a38f8c}{+ 2} = 3 \color{#a38f8c}{+ 2} \)
\( \phantom{-2+2\:\:} x = 5 \) Example 2
\( \phantom{\:\:- \frac{3}{5}m} \frac{3}{5}m + 2 = \frac{8}{5}m \)
\( \frac{3}{5}m \color{#a38f8c}{- \frac{3}{5}m} + 2 = \frac{8}{5}m \color{#a38f8c}{- \frac{3}{5}m} \)
\( \phantom{ \:\: \frac{3}{5}m - \frac{3}{5}m + } 2 = \frac{5}{5}m \)
\( \phantom{ \:\: \frac{3}{5}m - \frac{3}{5}m + } 2 = m \)
