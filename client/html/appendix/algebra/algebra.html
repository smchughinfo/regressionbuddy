Properties of Real Numbers

The following properties apply for any real numbers \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\):

Commutative properties of addition and multiplication \(a+b=b+a\)
\(ab=ba\)
Associative properties of addition and multiplication \((a+b)+c=a+(b+c)\)
\((ab)c=a(bc)\)
Distributive property \(a(b+c)=ab+bc\)
Identity properties of addition and multiplication \( a+0=a \)
\( a\cdot1=a \)
Inverse properties of addition and multiplication \( a-a=0 \)
\( a\cdot\frac{1}{a}=1 \)

Fractions

Multiplying Fractions To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators to get the numerator of the product, and multiply the denominators to get the denominator of the product.
Dividing Fractions To divide fractions, multiply the fraction in the numerator by the recriprocal of the fraction in the denominator.
Adding Fractions To add fractions, first rewrite them to have the same denominator. Then add the numerators to get the numerator of the product, and keep the denominator.

Multiplication Example \(\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{3}=\frac{2}{6}=\frac{1}{3}\)
Division Example \(\frac{\frac{1}{2}}{\frac{2}{3}}=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{3}{2}=\frac{3}{4}\)
Addition Example \(\frac{3}{2}+\frac{1}{4} =\frac{3}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{2}+\frac{1}{4} =\frac{6}{4}+\frac{1}{4} =\frac{7}{4}\)

Like Terms

Like terms are terms that have the same variables (including the exponent). Like terms can be combined.

Example 1 \(3xy+9xy=12xy\)
Example 2 \(x^2-2x^2={-x}^2\)
