The Multiplication Property of Equality If \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are real numbers, and \(c \neq 0\), then: \(a=b\) and \( ac=bc\) are equivelant equations. Example 1 \( \phantom{(-1)\;\;\:}-x=6 \)
\( \phantom{(-1)}(-1)x=6 \)
\( \color{#a38f8c}{(-1)}(-1)x=\color{#a38f8c}{(-1)}6 \)
\( \phantom{(1)\;\;\:}x=-6 \) Example 2
\( \phantom{3}\frac{1}{3} \cdot x=2 \)
\( \color{#a38f8c}{3}\frac{1}{3}x = \color{#a38f8c}{3\cdot}3 \)
\( \phantom{3}\frac{3}{3}x=\frac{9}{3} \)
\( \phantom{\frac{3}{3}3}x=3 \)
Example 3
\( .25m=3 \)
\( \phantom{.}\frac {.25m} {\color{#a38f8c}{.25}} = \frac {3} {\color{#a38f8c}{.25}} \)
\( \phantom{.25}m = .75 \)
