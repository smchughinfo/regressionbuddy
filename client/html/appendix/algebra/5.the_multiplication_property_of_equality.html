The Multiplication Property of Equality If \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are real numbers, and \(c \neq 0\), then: \(a=b\) and \( ac=bc\) are equivalent equations. Example 1 \( \phantom{\cdot(-1)}-x=6 \)
\( -x\color{#a38f8c}{\cdot(-1)}=6\color{#a38f8c}{\cdot(-1)} \)
\( \phantom{\cdot(-1)-\;}x=-6 \) Example 2
\( \phantom{3\cdot}\frac{1}{3} m=2 \)
\( \color{#a38f8c}{3\cdot}\frac{1}{3}m = \color{#a38f8c}{3\cdot}2 \)
\( \phantom{3\cdot}\frac{3}{3} m=6 \)
\( \phantom{3\cdot\;\;}m=6 \)
Example 3
\( \phantom{\frac{1}{4}\cdot} 4h=12 \)
\( \color{#a38f8c}{ \frac {1} {4} \cdot } 4h = \color{#a38f8c}{ \frac {1} {4} \cdot } 12 \)
\( \phantom{4\cdot} \frac {4} {4} h = \frac {12} {4} \)
\( \phantom{\frac{1}{4}\cdot\;} h = 3 \)
Example 4
\( .4x=3.2 \)
\( \frac {.4x} { \color{#a38f8c}{.4} } = \frac {3.2} {\color{#a38f8c}{.4}} \)
\( \phantom{.4} x=8 \)
