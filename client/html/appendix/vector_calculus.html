Vector Spaces

\(\mathbb{R}^1\)
\(\mathbb{R}^2\)
\(\mathbb{R}^3\)
\(\mathbb{R}^n\)
\(\mathbb{R}^1\), \(\mathbb{R}^2\), \(\mathbb{R}^3\), \(\mathbb{R}^4\), etc. are generally written as \(\mathbb{R}^n\). That is, \(\mathbb{R}^n\) may refer to \(\mathbb{R}^1\), \(\mathbb{R}^2\), \(\mathbb{R}^3\), \(\mathbb{R}^4\), etc.

Vector Addition and Scalar Multiplication

Vector Addition \( \left ( a_1^\phantom{1},\,a_2^\phantom{1},\,a_3^\phantom{1} \right) + \left ( b_1^\phantom{1},\,b_2^\phantom{1},\,b_3^\phantom{1} \right) = \left( a_1^\phantom{1} + b_1^\phantom{1} ,\, a_2^\phantom{1} + b_2^\phantom{1} ,\, a_3^\phantom{1} + b_3^\phantom{1} \right) \)
Scalar Multiplication \( k \left ( a_1^\phantom{1},\,a_2^\phantom{1},\,a_3^\phantom{1} \right) = \left ( ka_1^\phantom{1},\,ka_2^\phantom{1},\,ka_3^\phantom{1} \right) \)

Vector Addition Example
\(\mathbb{R}^2\)

\begin{align*} \boldsymbol{u} &= (-3,3) \\ \boldsymbol{v} &= (4,1) \\ \boldsymbol{u}+\boldsymbol{v} &= (1,4) \end{align*}
\(\mathbb{R}^3\)

\begin{align*} \boldsymbol{u} &= (-1,-1,2) \\ \boldsymbol{v} &= (3,0,1) \\ \boldsymbol{u}+\boldsymbol{v} &= (2,-1,3) \end{align*}
\(\mathbb{R}^n\)
  • \( (7) + (-3) =4 \)
  • \( (3, 8) - (9, 2) = (6, 6) \)
  • \( (5, 12, 0) + (-15, -11, 4) = (-10, 1, 4) \)
  • \( (3, 10, 1, 6) - (2, -6, 8, 0) = (1,16,-7,6) \)
Scalar Multiplication Example
\(\mathbb{R}^2\)
\begin{align*} k &= \text{-}\frac{1}{2} \\ \boldsymbol{u} &= (4,4) \end{align*}
\(\mathbb{R}^3\)

\begin{align*} k &= 2 \\ \boldsymbol{u} &= (1,1,1) \end{align*}
\(\mathbb{R}^n\)
  • \( 6(6) =36 \)
  • \( \frac{1}{2}(4, 2) = (2, 1) \)
  • \( 0(2, 11, 3) = (0, 0, 0) \)
  • \( 3(\frac{1}{3}, \frac{1}{3}, 1, 3) = (1, 1, 3, 9) \)
