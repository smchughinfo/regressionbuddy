Linear Algebra Appendix


Linear Equations in n Variables

Two Variables \(Ax+By=C\)
The line crosses the x axis at \(\frac{C}{A}\) when \(A \neq 0\). The line crosses the y axis at \(\frac{C}{B}\) when \(B \neq 0\). The slope of the line is \(-\frac{A}{B}\).
Three Variables \(Ax+By+Cz=D\)
The vector \((A,B,C)\) is normal (orthogonal, at a 90 degree angle) to the plane. The distance from the origin to point on the plane closest to the origin is \( \frac{\left|D\right|}{\sqrt{A^2+B^2+C^2}} \).
n Variables \(a_1^\hphantom{1} x_1^\hphantom{1} + a_2^\hphantom{1} x_2^\hphantom{1} +a_3^\hphantom{1} x_3^\hphantom{1} + \cdot \; \cdot \; \cdot \; + a_n^\hphantom{1} x_n^\hphantom{1} = b\)

The Number of Solutions to a System of Linear Equations

A solution of a system linear equations is a point that every equation in the system touches.

A system of linear equations can have no solutions, one solutions, or an infinite number of solutions.

  1. Systems with no solutions are called inconsistent.
  2. Systems that have a solution are called consistent.

Two Variables Example
No Solutions
\begin{align} x-y=1 \\ -x+y=1 \end{align}
One Solution
\begin{align} \hphantom{2}x-\hphantom{2}y &= 1 \\ 2x-2y &= 2 \end{align}
Infinite Solutions
\begin{align} \hphantom{2}x-\hphantom{2}y &= 1 \\ 2x-2y &= 2 \end{align}
Three Variables Example
No Solutions
\begin{align*} x+y+z &= 0 \\ x+y+z &= 2.5 \\ x+y+z &= 5 \end{align*}
One Solution
\begin{align} x+y+z &= 1 \\ x+y-z &= 1 \\ -\,x+y &= 1 \end{align}
Infinite Solutions \begin{align} x+y+z=1 \\ -x-y+z=1 \\ z=1 \end{align}
n Variables Example
No Solutions
\begin{align*} x_1^\phantom{1}-x_2^\phantom{1}+x_3^\phantom{1}-x_4^\phantom{1} &= 1\\ \phantom{x_1^1-x_2^1+}x_3^\phantom{1}+x_4^\phantom{1} &= 4\\ \phantom{x_1^1-x_2^1+}x_3^\phantom{1}\phantom{-x_4^1}\phantom{p} &= 2\\ \phantom{x_1^1-x_2^1+x_3^1+}x_4^\phantom{1} &= 1\\ \end{align*}

If \(x_4^\phantom{1}=1\) and \(x_3^\phantom{1}=2\) there is no way \(x_3^\phantom{1}+x_4^\phantom{1}\) can equal \(4\), so the system is inconsistent.

One Solution
\begin{align} x+y+z &= 1 \\ x+y-z &= 1 \\ -\,x+y &= 1 \end{align}
Infinite Solutions \begin{align} x+y+z=1 \\ -x-y+z=1 \\ z=1 \end{align}

Back-Substitution

Once you've found a variable in one equation you can use it in the other equations.

Back-Substitution Example
\(x+y+z=5\)
\(y+z=4\)
\(z=7\)

\(y+(7)=4\)
\(y=-3\)

\(x+(-3)+(7)=5\)
\(x=1\)
