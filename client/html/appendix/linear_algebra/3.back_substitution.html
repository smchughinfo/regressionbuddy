Back-Substitution Once you've found a variable in one equation you can use it in the other equations. Back-Substitution Example
We are given \(z=7\). \begin{align*} x+y+z &= 5 \\ \phantom{x+}y+z &= 4 \\ \phantom{x+y+}z &= 7 \Leftarrow \end{align*}
Substitute \(7\) for \(z\) and solve to get \(y=-3\). \begin{align*} x+y+ z &= 5 \\ \phantom{x+}y+ 7 &= 4 \Leftarrow \\ \phantom{x+y+}z &= 7 \end{align*}
Substitute \(7\) for \(z\) and \(-3\) for \(y\) and solve to get \(x=1\). \begin{align*} x -3 + 7 &= \phantom{-}5 \Leftarrow \\ \phantom{x-}y\phantom{+7}\;\, &= -3 \\ \phantom{x+y+}z &= \phantom{-}7 \end{align*}
Result: \begin{align*} x &= \phantom{-}1 \\ y &= -3 \\ z &= \phantom{-}7 \end{align*}
