Polynomial Curve Fitting

Through a process known as polynomial curve fitting a polynomial of degree \(n-1\) can be found that passes through a collection of \(n\) points.

Points
\((x_1,y_1),(x_2,y_2), . . . , (x_n,y_n) \)
System
\begin{align} a_0 + a_{1}x_1\; + a_{2}x_1^2\; + \cdots + a_{n-1}x_1^{n-1}\; &= y_1 \\ a_0 + a_{1}x_2\; + a_{2}x_2^2\; + \cdots + a_{n-1}x_2^{n-1}\; &= y_2 \\ &\;\; \vdots \\ a_0 + a_{1}x_n\; + a_{2}x_n^2\; + \cdots + a_{n-1}x_n^{n-1}\; &= y_n \end{align}
Polynomial
\( p(x)=b_0+b_1x+b_2x^2+\cdots+b_{n-1}x^{n-1} \)
Where the vector \( \mathbf{b}=(b_0, b_1, b_2, \cdots, b_{n-1}) \) is the solution of the system.
Exact Example Find a polynomial that fits the following points.
\( (-1,-4), (0,1), (1,0), (2,-1) \)
Create the system. 1 -1 -1^2 -1^3 -4; 1 0 0^2 0^3 1; 1 1 1^2 1^3 0; 1 2 2^2 2^3 -1 Solve the system using Gaussian elimination with back-substitution or Gauss-Jordan elimination.
\(a_1= 1\), \(a_2= 1\), \(a_3=-3\), \(a_4= 1\)
Create the polynomial.
\( p(x)=1+x-3x^2+x^3 \)
