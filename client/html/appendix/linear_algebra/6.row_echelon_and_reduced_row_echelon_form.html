Row-Echelon and Reduced-Row-Echelon Form

A matrix in row-echelon form has the following properties:

  1. Rows with all zeros are at the bottom of the matrix.
  2. Any row that's not all zeros has a leading 1.
  3. Every value below a leading 1 is 0.
A matrix is in reduced-row-echelon form if it's in row-echelon form and there is a zero above every leading 1. Row-Echelon Form Examples
