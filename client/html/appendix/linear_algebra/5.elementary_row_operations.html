Elementary Row Operations There are three elementary row operations which can be used to produce a different, but equivelant, system of equations. They are:
  1. Interchange two equations.
  2. Multiply an equation by a nonzero constant.
  3. Add one equation to another.
Interchange Two Equations
\( \left[ \begin{matrix} \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}1 & \phantom{-}7 & -5 \\ -3 & \phantom{-}3 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}2 \\ \phantom{-}2 & -1 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}8 \end{matrix} \right] \phantom{ \begin{matrix} \color{#a38f8c}{R_1 \leftrightarrow R_2} \\ \\ \\ \end{matrix} } \)

\( \left[ \begin{matrix} -3 & \phantom{-}3 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}2 \\ \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}1 & \phantom{-}7 & -5 \\ \phantom{-}2 & -1 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}8 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} \color{#a38f8c}{R_1 \leftrightarrow R_2} \\ \\ \\ \end{matrix} \)
Multiply by a Nonzero Constant
\( \left[ \begin{matrix} 7 & \phantom{-}0 & -1 & \phantom{-}4 \\ 0 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}5 & \phantom{-}1 \\ 9 & -3 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}6 \end{matrix} \right] \phantom{ \begin{matrix} \\ \\ \color{#a38f8c}{\frac{1}{3}R_3 \rightarrow R_3} \end{matrix} } \)

\( \left[ \begin{matrix} 7 & \phantom{-}0 & -1 & \phantom{-}4 \\ 0 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}5 & \phantom{-}1 \\ 3 & -1 & \phantom{-}0 & \phantom{-}2 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} \\ \\ \color{#a38f8c}{\frac{1}{3}R_3 \rightarrow R_3} \end{matrix} \)
