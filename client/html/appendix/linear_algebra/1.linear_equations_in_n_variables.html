Linear Equations in n Variables Two Variables \(Ax+By=C\)
The line crosses the x axis at \(\frac{C}{A}\) when \(A \neq 0\). The line crosses the y axis at \(\frac{C}{B}\) when \(B \neq 0\). The slope of the line is \(-\frac{A}{B}\). Three Variables \(Ax+By+Cz=D\)
The vector \((A,B,C)\) is normal (orthogonal, at a 90 degree angle) to the plane. The distance from the origin to point on the plane closest to the origin is \( \frac{\left|D\right|}{\sqrt{A^2+B^2+C^2}} \). n Variables The standard form of a linear equation is \( a_1^\hphantom{1} x_1^\hphantom{1} + a_2^\hphantom{1} x_2^\hphantom{1} +a_3^\hphantom{1} x_3^\hphantom{1} + \cdot \; \cdot \; \cdot \; + a_n^\hphantom{1} x_n^\hphantom{1} = b \). The coeffecients \(a_1^\phantom{1} \), \(a_2^\phantom{1} \), \(a_3^\phantom{1} \), . . . , \(a_n^\phantom{1}\) and the constant term \(b\) are real numbers.
