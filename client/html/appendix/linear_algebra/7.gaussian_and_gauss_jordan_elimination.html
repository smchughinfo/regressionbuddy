Gaussian and Gauss-Jordan Elimination Gaussian Elimination is the process of using elementary row operations to rewrite a matrix in row-echelon form. Gauss-Jordan Elimination is the process of using elementary row operations to rewrite a matrix in reduced row-echelon form. Gaussian Elimination Example Solve the system.
\begin{align} \phantom{2x_1+6} x_2 - \phantom{1\;\;}x_3 &= \phantom{-}4 \\ -2x_1 + 6x_2 - 12x_3 &= -4 \\ x_1 - 3x_2 + \phantom{1}7x_3 &= \phantom{-}7 \end{align}
Rewrite the system as an augmented matrix. 0 1 -1; 1 -3 6; 1 -3 7 \begin{matrix} \\ \color{#a38f8c}{-\tfrac{1}{2}R_2 \rightarrow R_2} \\ \\ \end{matrix} Use row operations to rewrite the matrix in row-echelon form. 1 -3 6 2; \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 -1 4; 1 -3 7 7 \begin{matrix} \color{#a38f8c}{R_1 \leftrightarrow R_2} \\ \\ \\ \end{matrix} 1 -3 6 2; \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 -1 4; \color{#a38f8c}{0} \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 5 \begin{matrix} \\ \\ \color{#a38f8c}{-R_1 + R_3 \rightarrow R_3} \end{matrix} Use back-substitution to find the solution.
\(x_1= -1\), \(x_2=9\), \(x_3=5\)
Gauss-Jordan Elimination Example Solve the system.
\begin{align} \phantom{2x_1+6} x_2 - \phantom{1\;\;}x_3 &= \phantom{-}4 \\ -2x_1 + 6x_2 - 12x_3 &= -4 \\ x_1 - 3x_2 + \phantom{1}7x_3 &= \phantom{-}7 \end{align}
Write the matrix in row-echelon form as was done in the Gaussian Elimination example. 1 -3 6 2; \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 -1 4; \color{#a38f8c}{0} \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 5   Use row operations to rewrite the matrix in row-echelon form. 1 0 3 14; \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 -1 4; \color{#a38f8c}{0} \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 5 \begin{matrix} \color{#a38f8c}{3R_2 + R_1 \rightarrow R_1} \\ \\ \\ \end{matrix} 1 0 3 14; \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 0 9; \color{#a38f8c}{0} \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 5 \begin{matrix} \\ \color{#a38f8c}{R_3 + R_2 \rightarrow R_2} \\ \\ \end{matrix} 1 0 0 -1; \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 0 9; \color{#a38f8c}{0} \color{#a38f8c}{0} 1 5 \begin{matrix} \color{#a38f8c}{-3R_3 + R_1 \rightarrow R_1} \\ \\ \\ \end{matrix} Read the solution off the matrix.
\(x_1= -1\), \(x_2=9\), \(x_3=5\)
