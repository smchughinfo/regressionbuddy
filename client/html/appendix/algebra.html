Properties of Real Numbers

For any real numbers \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\):

\(a+b=b+a\) Commutative properties of addition and multiplication
\(ab=ba\)
\((a+b)+c=a+(b+c)\) Associative properties of addition and multiplication
\((ab)c=a(bc)\)
\(a(b+c)=ab+bc\) Distributive property

Fractions

Multiplying Fractions

To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators to get the numerator of the product, and multiply the denominators to get the denominator of the product.

Dividing Fractions

To divide fractions, multiply one fraction by the recriprocal of the other fraction.

Adding Fractions

To add fractions, first rewrite them to have the same denominator. Then add the numerators to get the numerator of the product, and keep the denominator.


Multiplication Example
\(\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{3}=\frac{2}{6}=\frac{1}{3}\)
Division Example
\(\frac{\frac{1}{2}}{\frac{2}{3}}=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{3}{2}=\frac{3}{4}\)
Addition Example
\(\frac{3}{2}+\frac{1}{4}\) =\(\frac{3}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{2}+\frac{1}{4}\) =\(\frac{6}{4}+\frac{1}{4}\) =\(\frac{7}{4}\)

Like Terms

Like Terms

Like terms are terms that have the same variable (including its exponent). Like terms can be combined.


\(\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{2}{3}=\frac{2}{6}=\frac{1}{3}\)
