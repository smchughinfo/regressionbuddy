Trigonometric Functions at 30°, 45°, and 60° The trigonometric function values at 30°, 45°, and 60° are easy to derive or memorize. Derive at 45° /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-45-45.svg
Make a triangle with legs of length 1 and use the pythagorean theorem to compute the hypotenuse (\(\sqrt{2}\)). The trigonometric ratios at 45° can then be read off the triangle.
Derive at 30° and 60° /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-30-60.svg
Make an equilateral triangle with sides of length 2 and cut it in half. Use the pythagorean theorem to compute the length of the second leg (\(\sqrt{3}\)). The trigonometric ratios at 30° and 60° can then be read off the triangle.
Memorize
  30° 45° 60°
\(sin\) \(\frac{1}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)
\(cos\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{2}\)
\(tan\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\) \(1\) \(\sqrt{3}\)
30° Example /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-30-60.svg
\( sin(30°)=\frac{opp}{hyp}=\frac{1}{2} \)
45° Example /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-45-45.svg
\( csc(45°)=\frac{hyp}{opp}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{1}=\sqrt{2} \)
60° Example /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-30-60.svg
\( tan(60°)=\frac{opp}{adj}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{1}=\sqrt{3} \)
