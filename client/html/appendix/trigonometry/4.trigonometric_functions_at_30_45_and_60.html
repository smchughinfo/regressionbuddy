Trigonometric Functions at 30°, 45°, and 60° The trigonometric function values at 30°, 45°, and 60° are easy to derive or memorize.

Derive at 45°

 /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-45-45.svg
Make a triangle with legs of length 1. The length of the hypotenuse will therefore be:
\( 1^2+1^2=c^2 \)
\( 2=c^2 \)
\( c=\sqrt{2} \)
and the trigonometric ratios at 45° can be read off the triangle.
\( sin 45°=\frac{opp}{hyp}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \) \( cos 45°=\frac{adj}{hyp}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \) \( tan 45°=\frac{opp}{adj}=\frac{1}{1}=1 \)

Derive at 30° and 60°

 /images/trigonometry-appendix/triangle-30-60.svg
Make an equilateral triangle with sides of length 2 and cut it in half. The length of the new leg will therefore be:
\( 1^2+b^2=2^2 \)
\( 1+b^2=4 \)
\( b^2=3 \)
\( b=\sqrt{3} \)
and the trigonometric ratios at 30° and 60° can be read off the triangle.
\( sin 30°=\frac{opp}{hyp}=\frac{1}{2} \) \( cos 30°=\frac{adj}{hyp}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) \( tan 30°=\frac{opp}{adj}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \)
\( sin 60°=\frac{opp}{hyp}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) \( cos 60°=\frac{adj}{hyp}=\frac{1}{2} \) \( tan 60°=\frac{opp}{adj}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{1}=\sqrt{3} \)
Memorize
  30° 45° 60°
\(sin\) \(\frac{1}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)
\(cos\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{2}\)
\(tan\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\) \(1\) \(\sqrt{3}\)
