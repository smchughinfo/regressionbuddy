The Trigonometric Ratios

The six Trigonometric Ratios are:

 /images/trigonometry-appendix/basic-triangle.svg \( sin(\theta)=\frac{side\ opposite\ \theta}{hypotenuse} \) \( cos(\theta)=\frac{side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} {hypotenuse} \) \( tan(\theta)=\frac{side\ opposite\ \theta} {side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} \) \( csc(\theta)=\frac{hypotenuse} {side\ opposite\ \theta} \) \( sec(\theta)=\frac{hypotenuse} {side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} \) \( cot(\theta)=\frac{side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} {side\ opposite\ \theta} \) Example /images/trigonometry-appendix/1-sqrt3-2.svg \( sin(\theta)=\frac{1}{2} \) \( cos(\theta)=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) \( tan(\theta)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \) \( csc(\theta)=\frac{2}{1} \) \( sec(\theta)=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) \( cot(\theta)=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{1} \)
