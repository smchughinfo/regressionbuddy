Solving Right Triangles All angles and side lengths of a right triangle can be found given one of following conditions:
  1. An angle and a side length are known.
  2. Two side lengths are known.
 Angle and a Side Example /images/trigonometry-appendix/solving-right-triangles-example-1.svg
The missing sides can be found by setting up a trigonometric equation:
\begin{align} sin(53.13°) &= \frac{\color{#a38f8c}{\text{a}}}{1} \\ .8 &\approx \frac{\color{#a38f8c}{\text{a}}}{1} \\ .8 &\approx \color{#a38f8c}{\text{a}} \end{align}
The missing angle is the complement of the known angle.

\(\color{#a38f8c}{\text{C}}=90°-53.13°=36.87°\)
