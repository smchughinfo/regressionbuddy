Recriprocals of the Trigonometric Functions The recriprocal of a trigonometric function is another trigonometric function. Specifically: \( csc(\theta)=\frac{1}{sin(\theta)} \) \( sec(\theta)=\frac{1}{cos(\theta)} \) \( cot(\theta)=\frac{1}{tan(\theta)} \)

The six Trigonometric Functions are:

 /images/trigonometry-appendix/similiar-triangles.svg \( sin(\theta)=\frac{leg\ opposite\ \theta}{hypotenuse} \) \( cos(\theta)=\frac{leg\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} {hypotenuse} \) \( tan(\theta)=\frac{leg\ opposite\ \theta} {leg\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} \) \( csc(\theta)=\frac{hypotenuse} {leg\ opposite\ \theta} \) \( sec(\theta)=\frac{hypotenuse} {leg\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} \) \( cot(\theta)=\frac{leg\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} {leg\ opposite\ \theta} \) Example 1 Given \( sin(\theta)=\frac{3}{5} \), find \( csc(\theta) \).

\( csc(\theta) = \frac{1}{sin(\theta)} = \frac{1}{\color{#a38f8c}{\frac{3}{5}}} = \frac{5}{3} \) Example 2 Given \( cot(\theta)=\frac{7}{9} \), find \( tan(\theta) \).

\( cot(\theta) = \frac{1}{tan(\theta)} = \frac{1}{\color{#a38f8c}{\frac{7}{9}}} = \frac{9}{7} \)
