Measures of Central Tendency Mean is the sum of the values divided by the total number of values.
Sample Mean \( \overline{X} = \frac {X_1 + X_2 + X_3 + \cdots + X_4} {n} = \frac {\Sigma X} {n} \)
Population Mean \( \overline{\mu} = \frac {X_1 + X_2 + X_3 + \cdots + X_4} {N} = \frac {\Sigma X} {N} \)
Median is the value in the middle. Mode is the value that occurs most often. Midrange is the value half way between the largest and smallest value.
midrange \( =\frac{\text{largest + smallest}}{2} \)
Mean Example The number of green candies from ten different bags is recorded below. Find the mean.
\( 2, 3, 1, 5, 4, 16, 1, 2, 11, 3 \)

\( \overline{X} = \frac {2 + 3 + 1 + 5 + 4 + 16 + 1 + 4 + 11 + 3} {10} = \frac {50} {10} = 5 \) Median Example (odd) The number of hotdogs consumed by seven participants in a hotdog eating competition are recorded below. Find the median.
\( 5, 13, 9, 3, 5, 7, 10 \)
Step 1: Order the data. \( 3, 5, 5, 7, 9, 10, 13 \)
Step 2: Select the middle value. \( 3, 5, 5, \enclose{circle}[mathcolor="#a38f8c"]{7}, 9, 10, 13 \)
Median Example (even) The number of hotdogs consumed by eight participants in a hotdog eating competition are recorded below. Find the median.
\( 5, 13, 9, 3, 5, 7, 10, 12 \)
Step 1: Order the data. \( 3, 5, 5, 7, 9, 10, 12, 13 \)
Step 2: Select the middle value. \( 3, 5, 5, \enclose{circle}[mathcolor="#a38f8c"]{7, 9}, 10, 12, 13 \)
Step 3: Take the mean of the middle two values. \( \frac{7+9}{2} = \frac{16}{2} = 8 \)
Mode Example The number of cars owned in each of ten randomly selected households are recorded below. Find the mode.
\( 2, 2, 1, 2, 3, 1, 5, 0, 2, 3 \)
\(2\) occurs most often.
Midrange Example The number of pets owned in six different households is recorded below. Find the midrange.
\( 1, 0, 0, 1, 4, 0 \)
\( \frac{4+2}{2}= 3\)
