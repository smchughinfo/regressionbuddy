Levels of Measurement Nominal data can be categorized. Ordinal data can be categorized and ordered but differences in the data have little or no meaning. Interval data can be categorized and ordered. Differences in interval data are meaningful but the data has no natural zero. Ratio data can be categorized and ordered. Differences in ratio data are meaningful and the data has a natural zero. Nominal Data Examples Sex. Eye color. Favorite flavor of ice cream. Ordinal Data Examples Grade (A, B, C, D, F), Rating (1-5 stars), Ranking (1st place, 2nd place, etc). Interval Data Examples The Year. For example, there is no natural year 0. Other examples are IQ and temperature when it's not measured in the Kelvin scale. Ratio Data Examples Height. Weight. Distance traveled.
