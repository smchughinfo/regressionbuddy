Mean, Median, and Mode Mean is the sum of the values divided by the total number of values.
Sample Average \( \bar{X} = \frac {X_1 + X_2 + X_3 + \cdots + X_4} {n} = \frac {\Sigma X} {n} \)
Population Average \( \bar{\mu} = \frac {X_1 + X_2 + X_3 + \cdots + X_4} {N} = \frac {\Sigma X} {N} \) Median is the value in the middle. Mode is the value that occurs most often. Mean Example The number of green candies from ten different bags is recorded below. Find the mean.
\( 2, 3, 1, 5, 4, 16, 1, 2, 11, 3 \)
\( \bar{X} = \frac {2 + 3 + 1 + 5 + 4 + 16 + 1 + 4 + 11 + 3} {10} = \frac {50} {10} = 5 \) Median Example (odd) The number of hotdogs consumed by seven participants in a hotdog eating competition are recorded below. Find the mean.
\( 5, 13, 9, 3, 5, 7, 10 \)
Step 1: Order the data: \( 3, 5, 5, 7, 9, 10, 13 \)
Step 2: Select the middle value. \( 3, 5, 5, \enclose{circle}[mathcolor="#a38f8c"]{7}, 9, 10, 13 \) Median Example (even) The number of hotdogs consumed by eight participants in a hotdog eating competition are recorded below. Find the mean.
\( 5, 13, 9, 3, 5, 7, 10, 12 \)
Step 1: Order the data: \( 3, 5, 5, 7, 9, 10, 12, 13 \)
Step 2: Select the middle value. \( 3, 5, 5, \enclose{circle}[mathcolor="#a38f8c"]{7, 9}, 10, 12, 13 \)
Step 3: Take the mean of the middle two values. \( \frac{7+9}{2} = \frac{16}{2} = 8 \) Mode Example The number of cars owned in each of ten randomly selected households are recorded below. Find the mode.
\( 2, 2, 1, 2, 3, 1, 5, 0, 2, 3 \)
\(2\) occurs most often.
