The Trigonometric Ratios

The six Trigonometric Ratios are:

\( sin(\theta)=\frac{side\ opposite\ \theta} {hypotenuse} \)
\( cos(\theta)=\frac{side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} {hypotenuse} \)
\( tan(\theta)=\frac{side\ opposite\ \theta} {side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} \)
\( csc(\theta)=\frac{hypotenuse} {side\ opposite\ \theta} \)
\( sec(\theta)=\frac{hypotenuse} {side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} \)
\( cot(\theta)=\frac{side\ adjacent\ to\ \theta} {side\ opposite\ \theta} \)

Example
\( sin(\theta)=\frac{1} {2} \)
\( cos(\theta)=\frac{\sqrt{3}} {2} \)
\( tan(\theta)=\frac{1} {\sqrt{3}} \)
\( csc(\theta)=\frac{2} {1} \)
\( sec(\theta)=\frac{\sqrt{3}} {2} \)
\( cot(\theta)=\frac{\sqrt{3}} {1} \)
