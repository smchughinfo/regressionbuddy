The Line Through Two Points A line through two points can be expressed in point-direction form by subtracting the components of one point from the other. Example /images/vector-calculus-appendix/point-point-equation-of-a-line.png https://www.geogebra.org/3d/Y44AQzkc \begin{align} P &= (0,-2,2) \\ Q &= (2,2,1) \\ \\ \mathbf{a}&=(0,-2,2) \\ \mathbf{v}&=(Q_1-P_1,Q_2-P_2,Q_3-P_3)=(2,4,-1) \end{align}
