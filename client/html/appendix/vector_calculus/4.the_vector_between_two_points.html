The Vector Between Two Points Direction and Distance If the components of vectors \(\mathbf{a}\) and \(\mathbf{b}\) are equal to the coordinates of points \(A\) and \(B\), respectively, then the direction and distance from point \(A\) to point \(B\) is given by \(\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}\). When \(\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}\) starts from point \(A\) it joins \(A\) to \(B\) and is represented by the vector \( \overrightarrow{AB} \).
