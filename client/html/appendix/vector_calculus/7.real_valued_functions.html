Real-valued Functions Real-valued functions only output real numbers. To express that \(f\) maps \(A\) (a subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\)) onto \(\mathbb{R}^m\) we write:
\( f:A \subset \mathbb{R}^n \mapsto \mathbb{R}^m \) Scalar-valued Example \begin{align} &f: (x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4) \mapsto x_1+x_2-x_3+x_4 \\[4pt] &f: (5, 6, 7, 8) \mapsto 5+6-7+8 = 12 \end{align} Vector-valued Example \begin{align} &f: (x_1,x_2,x_3) \mapsto (x_1+x_2,x_1+x_3) \\[4pt] &f: (1,2,3) \mapsto (1+2,1+3) = (3,4) \end{align}
