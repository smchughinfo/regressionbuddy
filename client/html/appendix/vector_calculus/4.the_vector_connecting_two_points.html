The Vector Between Two Points Direction and Distance If the components of vectors \(\mathbf{a}\) and \(\mathbf{b}\) are equal to the coordinates of points \(A\) and \(B\), respectively, then the direction and distance from point \(A\) to point \(B\) is given by \(\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}\). When \(\mathbf{b}-\mathbf{a}\) starts from point \(A\) it joins \(A\) to \(B\) and is represented by the vector \( \overrightarrow{AB} \). Example /images/vector-calculus-appendix/vector-between-two-points.png https://www.geogebra.org/graphing/twYqbXxS \begin{align*} &A = \left(1,\tfrac{5}{2}\right) \\[2pt] &B = (5,3) \\[2pt] &B-A = \left(5-1,3-\tfrac{5}{2}\right)=\left(4,\tfrac{1}{2}\right) \end{align*}
