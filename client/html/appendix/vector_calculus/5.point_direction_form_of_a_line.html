Point-Direction Form of a Line To draw a line in space you need a point on the line and the direction of the line. Equation \( \mathbf{l}(t)=\mathbf{a}+t\mathbf{v} \) Coordinate Form \begin{align} x &= a_1+tv_1 \\ y &= a_2+tv_2 \\ z &= a_3+tv_3 \end{align} \begin{align} \mathbf{a} &= (a_1,a_2,a_3) =\text{a vector whose endpoint is on the line} \\ \mathbf{v} &= (v_1, v_2, v_3) =\text{the direction of the line} \\ t &= \text{takes on all real values} \end{align} *For lines in the plane drop the z component.
